Project Overview
Why This Dashboard Matters

Emergency rooms operate under constant pressure — limited beds, unpredictable patient volumes, and strict care time targets. Without real-time visibility into operational metrics, hospitals struggle to allocate staff efficiently or identify where patient experience breaks down.

This dashboard was built to give hospital administrators a clear, data-driven view of ER performance across 9,216 patient visits. By combining monthly snapshots with a consolidated multi-year view, it enables both day-to-day operational decisions and long-term strategic planning.

The analysis revealed that average wait times exceed the 30-minute target, that 40.68% of patients miss the timeliness benchmark, and that demand peaks sharply on Mondays and during late-night hours — all actionable findings for resource optimization.

Analysis
Key Findings
⚠️

Wait Time Target Missed

Average wait time of 35.3 minutes consistently exceeds the hospital's 30-minute benchmark. 40.68% of all patients across 19 months did not receive care within the target window.

📅

Peak Demand Patterns

Mondays (1,377 patients) are the busiest day, followed by Saturdays and Tuesdays. Peak hours cluster at 7 AM, 11 AM, 1 PM, and 11 PM — requiring proactive staffing plans.

🏥

Referral Distribution

58.6% of patients required departmental referrals. General Practice absorbed the largest share (1,840), followed by Orthopedics (995), Physiotherapy (276), and Cardiology (248).

👥

Demographics

Adults aged 30–39 form the largest cohort (1,200 patients). The patient population is racially diverse, with White (2,571), African American (1,951), Multiracial (1,557), and Asian (1,060) patients represented.

📊

Admission Patterns

Nearly half of all patients — 49.16% (4,612) — were admitted for inpatient care, while 50.84% were treated and discharged. The near-even split indicates high case severity in the ER population.

⭐

Satisfaction Scores

Average patient satisfaction sits at 4.99 out of 10, indicating moderate dissatisfaction. Combined with wait time overruns, this points to patient experience as a key improvement area.

Dashboard Design
Four-View Architecture
VIEW 01 — MONTHLY

Monthly Performance Monitor

Time-filtered view of all KPIs for a selected month. Tracks patient volumes, wait times, satisfaction scores, and referral patterns. Enables month-over-month comparison and quick operational status checks.

VIEW 02 — CONSOLIDATED

Full-Period Trend Analysis

Aggregated view across the entire Apr 2023–Oct 2024 dataset. Reveals long-term trends, seasonal patterns, and systemic performance gaps — designed for strategic and executive reporting.

VIEW 03 — PATIENT DETAILS

Granular Patient Records

Drill-down table with individual patient records: ID, demographics, wait time, referral department, and admission status. Supports case-level investigation and data validation.

VIEW 04 — KEY TAKEAWAYS

Executive Summary Page

Narrative analysis translating raw data into actionable insights for non-technical stakeholders. Covers wait time issues, peak periods, referral patterns, demographics, and admission outcomes in plain language.
